Planets


Planets are celestial bodies that orbit stars. In our solar system, there are eight recognized planets with diverse characteristics. Mercury is closest to the Sun, Venus is known for its thick atmosphere, Earth supports life, and Mars is a focus of exploration. Jupiter and Saturn are gas giants with prominent ring systems, while Uranus and Neptune are ice giants. Planetary science studies their formation, evolution, and potential habitability. Exoplanet discoveries expand our understanding of planetary diversity beyond our solar system.
