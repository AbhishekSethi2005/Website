Stars


A star is a luminous celestial object consisting mostly of hydrogen and helium undergoing nuclear fusion, which produces light and heat. Stars are the fundamental building blocks of galaxies, including our own Milky Way. They vary widely in size, mass, temperature, and brightness. The life cycle of a star is determined by its mass.
