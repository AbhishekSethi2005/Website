Moon


The moon is Earth's only natural satellite, a celestial body that orbits our planet. It is the fifth-largest moon in the solar system. The moon is approximately 1/6th the size of Earth and has a gravitational influence that affects tides. It has various phases, such as full moon, new moon, crescent moon, etc., depending on its position relative to the Earth and the Sun. The moon has been a subject of fascination and study throughout human history and is also a significant celestial object in various cultural and religious contexts.
